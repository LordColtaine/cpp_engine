1. The Physics Flex: 2D Fluid Simulation (SPH)
- How it works: Implement Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics (SPH). Particles calculate pressure and viscosity using Navier-Stokes equations to push each other apart.
- The Flex: Translating complex, real-world physics equations into raw C++ performance. Visually looks like realistic water splashing.

2. The Pathfinding Flex: Flow Field Swarm (Tower Defense)
- How it works: Re-purpose the Grid to hold a "Vector Field"—every cell points to the fastest route to the center. Spawn 100,000 entities that blindly ride the arrows.
- The Flex: Proves understanding of advanced RTS pathfinding, Graph Theory, and Dijkstra's algorithm. Simulates massive hordes like in 'They Are Billions'.

3. The Grid Flex: Falling Sand Engine (Cellular Automata)
- How it works: Ditch the GameObjects entirely. Every cell in the grid becomes a physical element (Sand falls, Water flows, Fire burns Wood).
- The Flex: Demonstrates highly optimized, cache-friendly bitwise operations to simulate millions of pixels at 144 FPS, similar to the game 'Noita'.

4. The AI Flex: Genetic Evolution
- How it works: Replace hardcoded unit stats with a digital "DNA" array. Units eat, reproduce, and pass on mutated DNA to automatically evolve optimized hunters.
- The Flex: Demonstrates a deep understanding of Genetic Algorithms, Unsupervised Machine Learning, and complex mathematical balancing.

5. The Math Flex: Boids (Flocking Simulation)
- How it works: Replace ants with "Birds" that use pure 2D vector math (Separation, Alignment, Cohesion) to fly together in emergent patterns.
- The Flex: Forces the creation of a Spatial Partitioning system (Quadtree/Hash Grid) to solve the O(N^2) collision bottleneck. A golden ticket for engine programming roles.
